Foreclosure and Bankruptcy in Minnesota

Facing foreclosure in Minnesota? Bankruptcy can stop the process immediately and give you time to save your home. Here is how non-judicial foreclosure works in Minnesota and how bankruptcy provides relief.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

How Foreclosure Works in Minnesota

Minnesota uses non-judicial foreclosure (foreclosure by advertisement), but provides a 6-month right of redemption after the sale. If the property is over 10 acres or if there is more than 12 months of payments owed, the redemption period may extend to 12 months.

Timeline: Non-judicial foreclosure in Minnesota takes approximately 2-3 months to the sale, plus the 6-month redemption period afterward.

How Bankruptcy Stops Foreclosure

The automatic stay under 11 U.S.C. section 362 immediately halts all foreclosure activity the moment you file bankruptcy. This includes:

  • Foreclosure lawsuits
  • Scheduled foreclosure sales
  • Sheriff's sales
  • Trustee's sales
  • Deficiency judgment actions

The stay applies even if the lender does not know about your filing yet. Once the lender is notified, any foreclosure action taken after filing is void.

Chapter 13: The Foreclosure Defense Tool

How Chapter 13 Saves Your Home

  • Stops foreclosure through the automatic stay
  • Catch up on missed payments over 3-5 years
  • Continue making regular monthly payments going forward
  • May strip off underwater second mortgages
  • Eliminate other debts to free up cash for mortgage

Minnesota Homestead Protection

  • Homestead exemption: $450,000
  • Protects your home equity in bankruptcy
  • Applies in both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13
  • Minnesota offers a strong homestead exemption of up to $450,000 for your primary residence. This generous protection means most Minnesota homeowners can keep their homes when filing bankruptcy.

Timing Is Critical

The sooner you act, the more options you have:

  • Early stage (first missed payments): Contact your lender about forbearance or loan modification before considering bankruptcy
  • Pre-foreclosure notice received: Consult with an attorney or legal aid about bankruptcy options
  • Foreclosure filed: Filing bankruptcy still stops the process, but you have less time
  • Sale date scheduled: Emergency bankruptcy filing can stop the sale, but must be filed before the sale occurs
  • After the sale: Options are very limited - in some states, a redemption period may still apply

Legal assistance: Mid-Minnesota Legal Aid and Volunteer Lawyers Network may provide free help with foreclosure defense and bankruptcy.

Can bankruptcy stop foreclosure in Minnesota?

Yes. Filing bankruptcy triggers the automatic stay, which immediately halts all foreclosure proceedings in Minnesota. Chapter 13 is especially powerful because it allows you to catch up on missed mortgage payments over 3-5 years while keeping your home.

How quickly do I need to file to stop a foreclosure sale?

The automatic stay takes effect the instant your bankruptcy petition is filed. Even if a foreclosure sale is scheduled for tomorrow, filing today stops it. Can I save my home in Chapter 7?

Chapter 7 can delay foreclosure temporarily through the automatic stay, but it does not provide a long-term mechanism to catch up on missed payments. Minnesota's $450,000 homestead exemption protects your equity, but you must be current on mortgage payments to keep the home. Chapter 13 is usually better for saving a home from foreclosure.
