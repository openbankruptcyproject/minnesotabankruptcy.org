Chapter 13 Bankruptcy in Minneapolis, Minnesota

Chapter 13 lets you reorganize your debts into a manageable 3-5 year repayment plan while keeping your property. Here is how it works for Minneapolis residents.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

How Chapter 13 Works

Unlike Chapter 7, Chapter 13 does not eliminate debts immediately. Instead, you propose a repayment plan to pay back some or all of your debts over 3-5 years. Key benefits:

  • Keep your home - catch up on missed mortgage payments through the plan
  • Keep your car - restructure car loan payments, potentially at a lower interest rate
  • No means test - income determines plan length, not eligibility
  • Protect co-signers - the co-debtor stay prevents creditors from pursuing co-signers
  • Cram down car loans - if your car loan is older than 910 days, you may reduce the balance to the car's value

Plan Length and Payments

Your plan length depends on your income relative to the Minnesota median:

Income LevelPlan LengthPayment Basis
Below Minnesota median3 yearsDisposable income after allowed expenses
Above Minnesota median5 yearsDisposable income after allowed expenses

Your monthly payment is based on your disposable income - the amount left after subtracting necessary living expenses from your income. Unsecured creditors receive whatever your disposable income can pay over the plan period.

Chapter 13 Timeline in Minneapolis

1File petition and proposed plan

Your attorney files your petition and proposed repayment plan with the D. Minn.. The automatic stay stops all collection actions.

2Begin plan payments (30 days)

You must begin making plan payments within 30 days of filing, even before the plan is confirmed.

3341 Meeting of Creditors (30-45 days)

Brief meeting where the trustee reviews your finances and plan.

4Confirmation hearing (2-3 months)

The court approves or modifies your plan. Creditors may object.

5Complete plan payments (3-5 years)

Make all plan payments through the trustee. Remaining qualifying unsecured debts are discharged.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Chapter 13 work in Minneapolis?

Chapter 13 lets you keep your property while repaying debts over 3-5 years through a court-approved plan. Cases are filed in the D. Minn..

How long is a Chapter 13 plan?

If your income is below the Minnesota median, your plan lasts 3 years. If above, it lasts 5 years.

Can I keep my house in Chapter 13?

Yes. Chapter 13 allows you to catch up on missed mortgage payments through your plan while keeping your home.

What is the Chapter 13 dismissal rate?

Chapter 13 dismissal rates vary. Nationally, about 33% of Chapter 13 cases are dismissed before completion. Working with an experienced attorney improves your chances of success.
