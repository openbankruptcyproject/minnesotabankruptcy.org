Can I Keep My Car in Bankruptcy?

Most Minneapolis residents can keep their vehicle when filing bankruptcy. Minnesota protects up to $5,000 of vehicle equity, and several options exist for car loans.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Minnesota Vehicle Exemption

Minnesota allows you to exempt up to $5,000 in equity in one motor vehicle. Equity means the car's current market value minus what you owe on it.

Example: Car worth $8,000, loan balance $6,000 = $2,000 equity. If $5,000 covers $2,000, the car is fully protected.

Options for Your Car Loan

Chapter 7 Options

  • Reaffirm: Sign a new agreement to keep paying. Keep the car.
  • Redeem: Pay the car's current value in one lump sum.
  • Surrender: Return the car. Remaining loan balance is discharged.

Chapter 13 Options

  • Continue payments: Pay through your plan. Catch up on arrears.
  • Cram down: If loan is 910+ days old, reduce balance to car's value.
  • Surrender: Return the car. Deficiency paid as unsecured debt.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I keep my car in bankruptcy in Minnesota?

In most cases, yes. Minnesota's vehicle exemption protects up to $5,000 of equity. If you have a car loan, you can usually keep the car by continuing payments.

What if I owe more than my car is worth?

If you are underwater on your car loan, you have no equity - the vehicle exemption is not even needed. In Chapter 13, you may be able to cram down the loan to the car's actual value if the loan is older than 910 days.

What happens to my car loan in Chapter 7?

You can reaffirm the debt (agree to keep paying) and keep the car, redeem the car by paying its current value in a lump sum, or surrender the car and discharge the loan.
